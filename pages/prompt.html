The Packet

A recent LLM model migration caused intent prediction in our AI-powered knowledge base to silently fail. Small gaps or ambiguity in intent descriptions can ripple through the system, leading to incorrect routing, misaligned behavior, and increased support issues. The previous ChatGPT model tolerated descriptions that relied on inference; the new model does not. This packet is the communication structure that surrounds the training built to fix it — three artifacts, each pitched to the audience that needs to act on it.

The Situation

The training is a 30-minute working session for Support Engineering. The packet surrounding it is the communication structure — the pieces written for the three audiences the training needs to land with in order to succeed.

The principle

The model follows what is written in the intent description, not what is implied.

Layered Communication

Enterprise enablement works best when each audience receives the version of the story they need to act on their role. This packet is structured in three tiers, progressing from business case to oversight to execution:

01
Leadership
WHY (Business Case)
This establishes the business 'why' and defines the ask for leadership support.
View Email
02
Management
WHY + HOW (Oversight)
This explains why the training is necessary and how to manage and reinforce team performance.

03
Support Engineering
HOW (Execution)
The tactical handout and reference guide for the engineers performing the daily work.
View Field Guide

Each artifact is self-contained. A leader can make a decision from the email alone. A manager has everything needed to reinforce the training afterward. An engineer has a reference they can return to while actually writing descriptions.

Three audiences. Three artifacts. Each tailored to convey the why and how.
