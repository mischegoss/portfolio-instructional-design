Hi [Leader],

Following our recent LLM model migration, our team noticed a silent degradation in intent prediction. Because the previous ChatGPT model was retired, the transition to a new LLM exposed a key gap: many of our existing prompts relied on the old ChatGPT model to infer context that was not explicitly defined in the intent definitions themselves.

The new model interprets instructions and description language more literally and is less likely to fill in missing context automatically, leading to intent failures.

I’d like to run a 30-minute training session for Support Engineering focused on adopting more model-agnostic and durable intent prediction descriptions using a structured prompt framework. This would improve consistency in intent prediction, while also helping the support team troubleshoot and resolve these types of tickets more quickly.

The outcome: Intent prediction reliability increases and descriptions are more durable and model-agnostic to prevent future failures.

Happy to walk you through more details if helpful, or I can proceed with scheduling.
